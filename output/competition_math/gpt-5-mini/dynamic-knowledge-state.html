Why write the extra line: - It demonstrates you actually performed the algebra, not just quoted a method. - It makes it easy for graders to follow and award partial credit if a small arithmetic mistake occurs later. - It prevents any ambiguity about which algebraic manipulation you used. How to do it concisely (one clear line that graders like): - Write the original expression, then on the next line show the multiplication by the conjugate and the simplified result. For example: (√(x+1) − 1)/x = [(√(x+1) − 1)(√(x+1) + 1)] / [x(√(x+1) + 1)] = x / [x(√(x+1) + 1)] = 1/(√(x+1) + 1) for x ≠ 0. - Then a final sentence: "Since 1/(√(x+1)+1) is continuous at 0 (domain x ≥ −1), the limit as x→0 is 1/2." That gives a compact but explicit "showing of work" and will satisfy most instructors.
